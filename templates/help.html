Help & Documentation

This page explains the features of the Lawrenceville Philanthropy Prediction tool, including the EDA graphs, prediction model, and frequently asked questions.

What do the EDA graphs mean?

  • Philanthropy by House: Shows the distribution of philanthropic engagement scores for each house. Useful for identifying houses with higher or lower average engagement.
  • Philanthropy by Gender: Compares philanthropic engagement across genders.
  • Philanthropy by Class Year: Visualizes engagement trends across different graduating classes.
  • Engagement Correlation Heatmap: Displays the correlation between various engagement metrics and philanthropy, helping to identify which factors are most related to giving.

About the Prediction Model

  • Model Type: Random Forest Classifier
  • Prediction Type: Binary (predicts whether an alumnus is likely to be philanthropic or not)
  • Model Accuracy: 0.99

FAQ

  • Q: What file format should I upload?
    A: Please upload an Excel file (.xlsx or .xls) with the required columns as shown in the sample file.
  • Q: What does a prediction of 1 or 0 mean?
    A: 1 means the model predicts the alumnus is likely to be philanthropic; 0 means not likely.
  • Q: How are the EDA graphs generated?
    A: The graphs are generated automatically from your uploaded data using Python and Seaborn.
  • Q: Is my data stored permanently?
    A: No, uploaded files and generated images are only kept temporarily for your session.
